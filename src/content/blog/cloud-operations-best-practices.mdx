# Cloud Operations Best Practices: Lessons From the Field

Migrating to the cloud is only the beginning. The real challenge — and the real value — lies in how you operate once you are there. After years of helping enterprises run production workloads on AWS, Azure, and hybrid environments, here are the practices we see making the biggest difference.

## 1. Treat Infrastructure as Code From Day One

Manual configuration is the enemy of repeatability. Whether you use Terraform, Pulumi, or cloud-native tools like AWS CDK, codifying your infrastructure ensures consistency across environments and makes disaster recovery a matter of re-running a pipeline rather than reconstructing from memory.

## 2. Implement Cost Governance Early

Cloud costs have a way of surprising organisations. Establish tagging policies, set up budget alerts, and review spend weekly — not quarterly. Reserved instances, spot instances, and right-sizing reviews should be part of your operational rhythm.

## 3. Observability Is Not Optional

Logs, metrics, and traces form the three pillars of observability. Invest in a unified observability stack — whether that is Datadog, Grafana, or cloud-native tools — and ensure every service emits structured telemetry from the start.

## 4. Automate Security and Compliance

Shift security left by embedding it into your CI/CD pipeline. Automated vulnerability scanning, policy-as-code with tools like OPA or AWS Config Rules, and regular access reviews keep your cloud environment secure without slowing down delivery.

## 5. Plan for Failure

Resilient systems are not built by hoping things will not break. Design for failure with multi-AZ deployments, automated failover, circuit breakers, and regular chaos engineering exercises.
